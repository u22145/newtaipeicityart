The performance "Resonating in New Art Land" deeply explores the local culture, history, and human charm of Yingge, where the art museum is located. It combines percussion and dance in a cross-disciplinary collaboration. The percussion ensemble led by the Ju Percussion Band utilizes ceramic percussion instruments and interprets music in various styles, incorporating multiple newly composed pieces. Anarchy Dance Theatre complements the performance with dynamic movements, creating a sensory tension between performers and audience, amplifying the abundant creative energy, and showcasing the vibrant pulse of New Taipei City that perfectly connects tradition and modernity.

Ju Percussion Group

Established in 1986, the Ju Percussion Group is Taiwan's first professional percussion ensemble that combines performance, education, research, and promotion to advance the development of modern percussion music. Over the years, the ensemble has been dedicated to sharing inspiring rhythms and has become a leading force in the world of percussion, blending traditional and contemporary styles in their performances and setting trends in the genre.

Anarchy Dance Theatre

Founded in 2010, Anarchy Dance Theatre is a performance company centered on modern dance. The creative process of which serves as a focal point for contemplating how we connect and relate to one another as humans. Anarchy also actively incorporates cutting edge technology in its creative productions, serving as a reflection on the potentials and limits of our use of technology. Working upon observations of the physical body in motion, the company builds its norm-breaking productions that explore time, space, objects, and the relationship between humans and their society. With its blend of conventional theater, dance technology and art installation, Anarchy has in recent years been a frequent guest at international arts and dance festivals, and was named as one of 2016’s dance companies of the year by the German dance magazine Tanz. Their 2019 production The Eternal Straight Line was nominated for the 18th annual Taishin Arts Award.
