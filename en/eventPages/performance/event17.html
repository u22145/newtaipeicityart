Have you heard it? The cultural seedlings nurtured by the soil are beginning to sprout, and the melodious music flows gently, resonating and reverberating in the New Art Land. The performance utilizes Hakka singing for interpretation, with the singer using gentle phrases like, “Do you want to play together (nˇ oi kiung ha loiˇ liau moˇ)?” inviting the audience to immerse themselves in the music, allowing their voices and hearts to relax.

CHIU SHU

Native Hakka language creation began in 2016 when Shu Chiu engaged in a dialogue between the land and music, incorporating the characteristics of theatrical performance. The goal was to gradually develop a unique Hakka musical style. Recently, Shu Chiu released her debut Hakka album titled “Waiting a present for,” which won the Golden Indie Music Award - Best Folk Album Category. She has also participated in theater performances such as “Big Band Species.”
