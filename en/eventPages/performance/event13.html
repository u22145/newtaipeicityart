Choreographer Huichen Tan opens the performance with "Poetry × Body × Song," selecting poems by contemporary Taiwanese female poets as the inspiration for the dance. The collaboration emphasizes the interconnectedness of the three elements, where poetry, dance, and music mutually influence and enrich each other. The poems serve as narratives of the self and the human experience, while the enchanting and spiritual voice of Hakka music singer Sirong Lo complements the dance, revealing the diverse and authentic inner worlds of the performers.

WATER REFLECTION DANCE ENSEMBLE

Established in 2001 and selected for further development by the Ministry of Culture of Taiwan and the New Taipei City, the dance company created works such as "East: Women Shifting on the Time Axis" (nominated for Best Dance at the Melbourne Fringe Festival), "Reflection of Mind" (selected as one of the Top Ten of the Year by Taisin Bank), "Nushu" (ranked among The Guardian's top three dance performances), "Flower of Life" (contemporary Hakka performance), and "Pollard Script" (selected to feature on the 12 Day of China Dance at the National Centre for the Performing Arts).
