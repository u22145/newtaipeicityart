11WITH LIGHT, SHIFTING
NEAR THE ENTRANCE OF YINGGE CERAMICS MUSEUM
LANDSCAPE INSTALLATION
COLORING ON BAMBOO STICKS
24 x 1.2 x 1.8 m

Following the extended sunlight, amidst graceful transitions, it connects the urban corridors and reflects the new stance of harmony between humans and nature. The artwork uses undyed bamboo sticks as the medium, which are first dyed and then arranged to form a vibrant gradient. The elusive reflection on the water's surface are constantly changing as the light moves throughout the day. It embodies a dialogue between folk craftsmanship and art, existing in a state of both connection and detachment. Just like the ever-evolving and changing nature of life, it echoes the essence of New Art Land.

YU WEN-FU

Born in Yunlin, Taiwan in 1968, the artist has been actively engaged in various forms of art such as painting, sculpture, environmental art, and landscape art since youth. Blending tradition with contemporary contexts, the artist explores the use of bamboo, symbolizing their hometown, and feathers, representing the pursuit of dreams. These materials intertwine and interact, symbolizing roots and flight. The artist’s recent works have been showcased in art festivals in locations such as Nagano, Japan, Kinmen, Taitung, Taoyuan, and the north coast of Taiwan.
