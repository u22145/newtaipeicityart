04WEAVING ROUTE
SANYING SPATIAL ART DISTRICT
WOVEN-TYPE LANDSCAPE INSTALLATION
RECYCLED YARN / GALVANIZED STEEL FRAME
12 x 7 x 4 m

A large-scale spatial structure constructed using metal frames and recycled yarn, which responds to the environment and creates interconnected relationships between artworks. By employing weaving techniques, soft and hard materials support and produce each other, dancing together under the sunlight, showcasing the industrial aesthetics of collaboration between humans and the land. The tactile weaving process further embodies the craftsmanship of New Taipei, creating a layered environment where people and materials intersect.

ARCHIBLUR LAB

Established in 2015, this practice explores the “empathy” between the body and the landscape and the relationship between architectural generation through creative expression. It presents an alternative way of viewing and placing the body within the context. Notable works include the “Urban Archipelago Project,” “Urban Archipelago Project ,” “Light Ponds,” “Floating Field,” the 2016 Taipei Biennial, the “Collectivism” from the 2017 Sharjah Biennial and the Palestinian Museum’s “Boundaries of Existence.”
