13BLACK DREAM
EARTH PASSAGE
PARTICIPATORY LARGE-SCALE SCULPTURE
STYROFOAM／ ENVIRONMENTALLY-FRIENDLY PAINT
4 x 4 x 2.5 m TOTAL 5 PIECES

Breaking free from everyday logic, the black objects scattered across the land like ruins serve as interactive sculptures that allow viewers to freely assemble and move them, responding to the vision of a participatory art museum for all. These large-scale, eco-friendly painted Styrofoam structures, composed of five sets, are the products of our childhood fantasies. As they are stacked and combined, they resemble the construction of a colossal deity, transforming the process into a shared belief in co-creation. They invite people to pause, look up, and gaze in awe.

CHIANG CHUNG-LUN ＆ LIN CHIEN-CHIH

Chiang, Chung-Lun
Born in 1979 in Taichung, creates works in various forms that bring out the extraordinary power within the ordinary aspects of daily life.

Lin, Chien-Chih
Born in 1985 in Yunlin, primarily works with wood as his main creative medium. In his early works, he explored the connection between form vocabulary and consumer culture, while in recent years, he has focused on the possibilities presented by the surplus materials generated during the creative process.

Their collaborative work, “Black Dream,” has been exhibited in recent years at the Museum of Contemporary Art Taipei’s “Multiple Universes” and the Taipower Company’s Public Art Festival, “Electri City.”
