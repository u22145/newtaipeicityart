WATER FOUNT
RECYCLED ASSEMBLY, PARTICIPATORY INSTALLATION
STEEL／WOOD／PAINT／STREETLIGHTS／TREES.
Ø 3.2 x 3.9 m

This art installation, inspired by the memory of New Taipei City’s industry, is created using recycled materials and takes the form of a small mountain nestled within a forest. Embracing a small tree, it provides a peaceful corner for environmental rest. The hollow openings on the mountaintop allow gentle light to enter, casting a soothing glow on the surroundings. “Humans” and “trees” can quietly rest in the play of light and shadow, symbolizing a space for spiritual rejuvenation. It carves out a moment in time, offering a contrast to the bustling city and exploring the potential for interaction between objects and people.

LI CHENG–LIANG

The artist, born in 1986 in Keelung, initially explored the possibilities of art with a nonsensical and absurd approach. In 2013, The artist began working in Tainan, integrating craftsmanship into their conceptual works. The artist’s choice of medium is unrestricted, and the artist develops collaborations between crafts and art, responding to object forms and environmental spaces. This artist focuses on the present moment of life and seeks interpretations of living conditions. The artist’s works include “Outer Space and Moon Series,” “The Wannian Station on the Road,” and “Dio Camel” (winner of the 2022 Kaohsiung Award).
