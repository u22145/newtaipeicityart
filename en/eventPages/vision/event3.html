INFLATABLE INSTALLATION
12 x 8 x 7.5 m

Between reality and imagination, the artwork and the upcoming New Taipei City Art Museum building are juxtaposed, forming a repeated and continuous symbolic connection, constructing a vast attitude of adventure in the New Art Land. This piece, constructed by stacking inflatable elements, is a communal and immersive castle that invites everyone to embark on a journey, explore, and open themselves to the perception of light, shadow, and changing scales, moving towards the future.

CYRIL LANCELIN

Born in 1975, the french artist develops a hybrid work made up of sculptures, immersive installations, drawings, virtual experiences and videos that forge links between the physical and the fictional.

It is from a plastic vocabulary based on primitive geometry that he links architecture and the human body, the everyday and the functional, the perennial and the ephemeral, science and nature.The notions of repetition and parametric generation are recurring themes in his work. It anticipates our passage into a world of multiplied and shared data.

Digital or real, his works offer an essentially optimistic vision, drawing an artificial and experiential landscape. His work has been exhibited worldwide including Paris, Barcelona, Dubai, New York, Philadelphia, Beijing, and Toronto.
