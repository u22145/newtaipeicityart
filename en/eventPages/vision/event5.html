05ISLANDS
EAST FIELD
PERFORMATIVE SPATIAL INSTALLATION (CONTINUOUSLY CONSTRUCTED DURING THE EXHIBITION PERIOD)
CONSTRUCTION／MIXED MEDIA
DEPENDS ON THE VENUE TOTAL 5 PIECES

Echoing the consciousness of infinite cycles and creation of the “New Land,” the artwork is both an interactive and ongoing performative structure and an event of migration and relocation. The artist and the co-creators from Taiwan live together on the “island” during the exhibition period and move towards the next island. The original island gradually disintegrates, leaving only its skeleton, and the fragments of this disintegration are then used to construct bridges and pathways leading to the next island. Throughout the process, there is no contact with the ground, and new co-creators join the journey, forming a diverse community and embodying the collaborative energy of constant renewal and mutual transmission in the New Land.

ALEX SCHWEDER & WARD SHELLEY

For more than a decade, the duo have been practicing a form of experimental architecture that explores the dance between the designed environment and its consequences. They have designed, built, and lived in (or on) structures in locations where the public are invited to witness and to actively engage with the artists in direct dialogue — an activity that has coalesced into what they call “performance architecture.” Blurring the boundaries between art, architecture, design, and performance, the artists’ work poses questions about the nature of social space and the way architecture influences human behavior.
Their work has been collected and exhibited internationally including the Museum of Modern Art, The Aldrich Contemporary Art Museum, and Performa 17. （Photo Credit｜Dora Somosi）
